When a homeowner decides they want a cleaning service, they typically reach out to two or three companies. The one that responds within minutes — not hours — gets the booking in most cases. A potential client asks for a quote on a move-out cleaning. You text them a price while you're between jobs. They say "let me check with my landlord" or "I need to confirm the date." Without automated follow-up, that quote dies in silence about 60% of the time. GHL runs a multi-step text sequence that re-engages them over the next several days — no manual effort required.

Google Maps results dominate local cleaning searches. Your ranking depends on review count, average rating, and recency. A cleaning company with 150 reviews and a 4.8 average will show up above one with 25 reviews and a 5.0. GHL automates review requests after every completed job, adding reviews steadily without anyone on your team asking in person.

One-Time Clients Are Wasted Without Conversion

The most profitable cleaning companies have a high percentage of recurring clients. Weekly and biweekly accounts provide predictable revenue, efficient routing, and lower customer acquisition costs. But most one-time deep clean customers are never pitched on a recurring plan. GHL automates this conversion with a post-service sequence that introduces recurring options at the exact moment the customer is most impressed — right after a thorough cleaning.

How to Set Up GoHighLevel for Your Cleaning Company

Start with the four systems that have the fastest revenue impact: pipeline, missed call text-back, quote follow-up, and review automation. Everything else layers on top.

Step 1: Build Your Cleaning Pipeline

Your pipeline is a visual board showing every lead and where they are in your sales process. For a cleaning company:

  1. New Inquiry — someone called, texted, filled out a form, or messaged through Google
  2. Quote Sent — you've provided pricing (flat rate or after a walkthrough)
  3. Booked — date and time confirmed, on the calendar
  4. Completed — cleaning finished, review request fires
  5. Recurring Pitch — one-time client enters the recurring conversion sequence
  6. Recurring Active — client is on a weekly, biweekly, or monthly plan
  7. Lost — lead chose a competitor or went unresponsive

In GHL, go to Opportunities, click "Add Pipeline," and name it "Cleaning Jobs." If you handle both residential and commercial accounts, create separate pipelines. Commercial cleaning has longer sales cycles, involves property managers or office managers rather than homeowners, and often requires site visits before quoting. Keeping them separate makes both pipelines cleaner and easier to manage.

Step 2: Turn On Missed Call Text-Back

Go to GHL Settings, find "Missed Call Text Back," and set up your automatic message. A good text for cleaning companies: "Hi, this is [Company Name]! Sorry we missed your call — our team is at a job site right now. Text us what you need (house cleaning, deep clean, move-out, commercial) and we'll get back to you with availability and pricing shortly!"

The customer texts back: "Looking for biweekly house cleaning, 3 bed 2 bath." GHL creates a contact, logs the conversation, adds them to your pipeline, and notifies you. When you finish your current job, you respond with a quote and your booking link. That lead is now yours instead of the competitor who happened to answer the phone.

For cleaning companies, the morning and early afternoon are the busiest calling periods — homeowners tend to reach out during business hours while the owner and team are already on jobs. Missed call text-back is critical during these peak hours.

Step 3: Build Your Quote Follow-Up Sequence

Most cleaning quotes are given over text or phone. The customer says they'll think about it. Here's a proven follow-up sequence that runs automatically in GHL:

  • Day 0 (same day): Text — "Hey [Name], here's a recap of the quote we discussed for your [type of cleaning]. [Price] for a [frequency/type]. Want to pick a date? Here's our booking link: [link]. Happy to answer any questions!"
  • Day 2: Text — "Hi [Name], just checking in on your cleaning quote. We have a few openings this week if you'd like to get on the calendar. Reply here or book directly: [link]"
  • Day 5: Email — Detailed email with your quote summary, what's included, before/after photos from recent jobs (social proof), and a booking link. Include your Google review rating ("Rated 4.9 stars by 200+ homeowners").
  • Day 7: Text — "Last check-in on your cleaning quote, [Name]. The price is locked in for 30 days. No pressure — whenever you're ready, here's the booking link: [link]. We'd love to earn your business."

When someone books, move them to "Booked" in the pipeline, and the follow-up sequence stops automatically. This simple automation can increase your quote-to-booking rate by 20-30% — which for most cleaning companies means several additional bookings per month without lifting a finger.

Step 4: Automate Review Requests

Create a workflow that triggers when a contact moves to "Completed" in your pipeline:

  • Wait: 3 hours after completion (gives the client time to walk through and enjoy the clean house)
  • Send text: "Hey [Name], hope your home looks and feels amazing! If you're happy with the cleaning, a quick Google review would mean the world to our team. It really helps other families in [City] find us. Here's the link: [Google review link]. Thank you so much!"
  • Wait: 4 days
  • Send text: "Quick reminder — if you have a minute, we'd love a review. Helps us keep growing and serving families like yours. [link]"

The timing is key. Three hours after completion, the house is still spotless and the customer is at peak satisfaction. That's when they're most likely to leave a glowing review. Cleaning companies running this system consistently add 8-15 new reviews per month, which directly impacts Google Maps visibility and booking volume.

Step 5: Build the One-Time to Recurring Conversion Sequence

This is where the real profitability lives. A one-time deep clean might bring in $200-400, but a biweekly recurring client at $150/visit is worth $3,900 per year. Converting even a small percentage of one-time clients into recurring accounts transforms your business.

Create a workflow that triggers 24 hours after a one-time cleaning is completed:

  • Day 1: Text — "Hey [Name], we loved cleaning your home yesterday! Quick question — have you ever considered a regular cleaning schedule? Our biweekly clients get a discounted rate and priority scheduling. Want me to put together a recurring plan for you?"
  • Day 3: Email — Breakdown of recurring plans (weekly, biweekly, monthly), pricing for each, what's included in each visit, and benefits (priority scheduling, locked-in rate, same team every visit). Include a testimonial from a current recurring client.
  • Day 5: Text — "Just following up — here's what our biweekly plan looks like for a home like yours: [price]/visit, same team every time, and you never have to think about scheduling again. Want to try it out? We can start with two visits and see how you like it."
  • Day 10: Text — "Last thought on this — our recurring clients love the consistency and the savings. If the timing isn't right now, no worries at all. We'll be here whenever you're ready. [booking link]"

The "try it out" offer in the Day 5 message is powerful. It lowers the commitment barrier — the customer isn't signing a contract, they're just trying two visits. Once they experience the convenience of a clean house every two weeks without thinking about it, most convert to long-term recurring clients.

Each of these targets search intent from people who are actively considering hiring a cleaner.

Combine Magai for content creation with GHL for capturing leads from that content. Seasonal Campaigns That Keep Bookings Consistent

Cleaning demand fluctuates throughout the year, but smart campaigns can smooth out the slow periods:

Spring (March-May)

  • Spring deep cleaning packages — the biggest cleaning season of the year
  • Post-winter refresh — windows, baseboards, blinds, and areas neglected during cold months
  • Allergy season cleaning — HEPA vacuuming, dusting, air vent cleaning

Summer (June-August)

  • Move-out/move-in season — college students, military PCS, apartment turnovers
  • Vacation prep/return cleaning — "Come home to a spotless house"
  • Airbnb/short-term rental turnover services

Fall (September-November)

  • Back-to-school reset — getting the house organized after summer chaos
  • Pre-holiday deep clean — prepare for Thanksgiving and holiday guests
  • Window cleaning before winter (last chance for exterior work)

Winter (December-February)

  • Post-holiday cleaning — tree needles, decorations, entertaining aftermath
  • New Year reset — start fresh with a deep clean
  • Gift certificates — cleaning as a gift for new parents, busy professionals, elderly family members

For each campaign, create a GHL workflow that sends a targeted text or email blast to the right customer segment. Spring cleaning offers go to all past customers. Move-out specials target contacts tagged as "renter" or "apartment." Holiday campaigns go to your full list. Segment and time them right, and seasonal dips become seasonal spikes.
