What Roofing Contractors Actually Need in Email Software

Most "best email marketing" lists are written by people who've never worked with contractors. Here's what actually matters for roofing companies:

1. Speed-to-Lead Automation

The roofing company that responds first wins 50% of jobs. Your email software needs to trigger instant responses—ideally paired with SMS.

2. SMS Integration

Homeowners don't check email when their roof is leaking. They check texts. Any platform without SMS is leaving money on the table.

3. Review Request Sequences

Google reviews = more organic leads. The best platforms automatically request reviews 2-3 days after job completion.

4. Storm/Event Triggers

When a hailstorm hits, you need to blast your entire database immediately. Look for platforms that make this one-click simple.

5. Pipeline/CRM Integration

Email open rates mean nothing if you can't track which leads turned into closed jobs.

Email Marketing Templates for Roofing Contractors

New Lead Follow-Up Sequence

  1. Email 1 (Instant): "Thanks for reaching out! Here's what happens next..."
  2. Email 2 (Day 1): Before/after photos from recent local job
  3. Email 3 (Day 3): Customer testimonial + scheduling reminder
  4. Email 4 (Day 7): "Still need help with your roof?" + limited-time offer

Post-Job Review Request

  1. Day 2: "How'd we do? Quick favor to ask..."
  2. Day 5 (if no review): "Your feedback helps other homeowners find us"
  3. Day 10 (final): "Last chance to leave your review"

Common Email Marketing Mistakes Roofers Make

  • Waiting too long to follow up: If you're not responding within 5 minutes, you're losing jobs
  • Email-only sequences: Always pair with SMS for 3x higher response rates
  • Generic templates: Mention the customer's address, roof type, or local area
  • No tracking: If you don't know which emails lead to booked jobs, you can't improve
  • Ignoring past customers: Your database is a goldmine for referrals and repeat business
