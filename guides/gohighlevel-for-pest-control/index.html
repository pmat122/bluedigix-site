Why Pest Control Companies Need a CRM Like GoHighLevel

Pest control shares some characteristics with other service businesses but has its own dynamics that make the right CRM and automation platform especially valuable.

Emergency Response Speed Determines Revenue

Unlike HVAC or plumbing where some calls are routine, a large percentage of pest control inquiries are panic-driven. The homeowner just saw a mouse, found termite damage, or woke up covered in bites. They want someone there today or tomorrow. If your phone goes to voicemail while you're doing a treatment, they call the next company on the list. GHL's missed call text-back responds instantly, keeping the lead engaged until you can talk.

Quarterly Plans Are the Business Model

One-time treatments are valuable, but the business model that scales is recurring quarterly service. A customer paying $75-150 per quarter for preventive pest control is worth $300-600/year with minimal sales cost after the initial conversion. The challenge is twofold: converting one-time customers to quarterly plans and preventing existing quarterly customers from letting plans lapse. Both require automated follow-up that most pest control companies don't have.

Seasonality Creates Revenue Swings

Spring and summer are peak season — ants, mosquitoes, wasps, spiders, termites. Fall and winter slow down significantly in most markets. Without proactive campaigns targeting rodents (fall/winter), stored product pests, and pre-season prevention, your winter months can see a 40-60% revenue drop. GHL lets you run seasonal campaigns that smooth out these dips.

Reviews Control Local Search Visibility

When someone searches "pest control near me" or "exterminator [city]," Google Maps results dominate. Your ranking depends heavily on review volume and recency. A pest control company does dozens of treatments per week — each one is an opportunity for a review. Without an automated system requesting reviews after every treatment, you're leaving your Google visibility on the table.

How to Set Up GoHighLevel for Your Pest Control Company

Deploy the four highest-impact systems first: pipeline, missed call text-back, quote follow-up, and review automation. Then layer on quarterly reminders, one-time to recurring conversion, and seasonal campaigns.

Step 1: Build Your Pest Control Pipeline

Your pipeline stages should mirror the actual customer journey:

  1. New Lead — someone called, texted, filled out a form, or messaged you online
  2. Contact Made — you've spoken with them and understand the pest issue
  3. Quote Sent — you've provided pricing for treatment or a quarterly plan
  4. Scheduled — the initial treatment is on the calendar
  5. Treatment Complete — first service done; review request fires and quarterly conversion sequence begins
  6. Quarterly Active — customer is on a recurring prevention plan
  7. Lost — went with a competitor or went unresponsive

If you handle both residential and commercial pest control, create separate pipelines. Commercial accounts — restaurants, hotels, property management companies, food processing facilities — have different sales cycles, compliance requirements, and service frequencies. A restaurant might need monthly service with documentation for health inspections. A homeowner needs quarterly prevention. Keep them separate so each pipeline moves at its natural pace.

Step 2: Turn On Missed Call Text-Back

In GHL Settings, configure your automatic missed call response: "Hi, this is [Company Name]. Sorry we missed your call — our technicians are on service calls right now. Text us what pest issue you're dealing with and the address, and we'll get back to you ASAP with availability and pricing."

A customer texts back: "Seeing a lot of ants in the kitchen, finding them near the sink every morning. 123 Oak Street." GHL creates the contact, logs the conversation, adds them to your pipeline, and notifies you. When you finish your current treatment, you respond with next-day availability and a quote. That lead is now yours.

The urgency factor makes missed call text-back especially powerful for pest control. Someone with a pest problem isn't casually browsing — they want action now. An instant text response buys you the time to call back without losing them to a competitor.

Step 3: Build Your Quote Follow-Up Sequence

Pest control quotes often involve larger tickets — termite treatments can run $800-2,000+, quarterly plans represent a commitment. Some customers need a few days to decide. Here's an effective follow-up sequence:

  • Day 0: Text — "Hey [Name], thanks for reaching out about the [pest type] issue at your home. Here's a recap of what we discussed: [treatment type] at [price]. We have availability this week if you'd like to get it taken care of. Want me to hold a spot?"
  • Day 2: Text — "Just checking in, [Name]. We still have openings this week for your [pest type] treatment. The sooner we treat, the less chance of the problem spreading. Reply or book here: [link]"
  • Day 4: Email — Detailed email with treatment plan, what to expect during and after treatment, guarantee information, and before/after photos. Include your Google rating as social proof.
  • Day 7: Text — "Last follow-up on your pest control quote, [Name]. The pricing is locked for 30 days. Pests rarely resolve on their own — we're here whenever you're ready. [booking link]"

The "pests rarely resolve on their own" line on Day 7 is effective because it's true and creates natural urgency. Unlike a roof that can wait, a pest problem tends to get worse with time. The follow-up is helpful, not pushy.

Step 4: Automate Review Requests

Pest control companies have a natural advantage for review collection: high service volume. If you're doing 15-25 treatments per week, that's 15-25 review request opportunities. Set up a workflow:

  • Trigger: Contact moves to "Treatment Complete"
  • Wait: 4 hours (gives the customer time to verify the pest issue is addressed)
  • Send text: "Hey [Name], hope we got those [pest type] taken care of for you! If you're happy with the service, a quick Google review would really help other homeowners in [City] find reliable pest control. Here's the link: [review link]. Thank you!"
  • Wait: 4 days
  • Send text: "Quick reminder — if you have a minute, a Google review means a lot to our small team. [review link]"

With 15+ treatments per week and even a 25% review response rate, you're adding 15-20 new Google reviews per month. Over a year, that's 180-240 reviews — which puts you in dominant position in local search results.

Step 5: Build the One-Time to Quarterly Conversion Sequence

This is the most valuable automation for pest control companies. A one-time treatment might be $150-300. A quarterly customer is worth $600-1,200 per year. Converting even 20% of one-time customers to quarterly plans dramatically changes your revenue trajectory.

Create a workflow that triggers 24 hours after a one-time treatment is completed:

  • Day 1: Text — "Hey [Name], hope the [pest type] treatment is working well. Quick question — did you know we offer quarterly prevention plans? We treat your home every 3 months to keep pests from coming back. Most homeowners find it's cheaper than dealing with infestations one at a time. Want me to put together a plan for your property?"
  • Day 4: Email — Breakdown of quarterly plan: what's included in each visit (interior perimeter, exterior perimeter, targeted treatment), price per quarter, guarantee details, and the math showing quarterly prevention costs less than 2-3 one-time emergency calls per year.
  • Day 6: Text — "Following up on the quarterly plan, [Name]. At [price]/quarter, you get year-round protection and priority scheduling. If you see any pest activity between visits, we come back at no charge. Want to start? [booking link]"
  • Day 14: Text — "Last note on this — our quarterly customers rarely deal with the kind of [pest type] situation you just had, because prevention keeps them ahead of it. Whenever you're ready, the offer stands. [link]"

The guarantee is a strong selling point. Most pest control companies already offer between-visit callbacks at no charge for quarterly customers. Emphasizing this in the automation removes risk from the customer's perspective.

Growing Beyond Residential: Targeting Commercial Accounts

Residential pest control is the bread and butter, but commercial accounts offer higher contract values and more predictable revenue. Restaurants, hotels, food processing plants, property management companies, and warehouses all need regular pest control — and many are required by health codes to have a documented pest management program.

Tools like Apollo let you build targeted lists of local commercial prospects. Filter by industry (restaurants, hospitality, food service, property management), employee count, and location to find businesses within your service area that likely need pest control but may not have a current provider — or may be unhappy with their current one. Combined with GHL for managing the outreach and follow-up, you can systematically pitch commercial pest management contracts to businesses that represent $2,000-10,000+ per year in recurring revenue.

The outreach sequence in GHL might look like: introductory email about your commercial pest management program, follow-up text offering a free inspection, and a third touch with a case study from a similar business type. Commercial accounts take longer to close but deliver significantly higher lifetime value than residential.

Seasonal Pest Campaigns That Fill Your Calendar Year-Round

Every pest has a season. Smart campaigns that align with the local pest calendar keep your schedule full even during traditionally slow months:

Spring (March-May)

  • Ant prevention — treat before colonies expand
  • Termite inspection season — peak swarm activity alerts homeowners
  • Mosquito treatment programs — recurring yard treatments begin
  • Spring cleanout special — general pest prevention after winter

Summer (June-August)

  • Wasp and hornet nest removal — peak nesting season
  • Tick and flea treatments — especially for homes with pets and yards
  • Spider control — high visibility drives inquiries
  • Fire ant treatments (southern markets)

Fall (September-November)

  • Rodent exclusion — mice and rats seek shelter as temperatures drop
  • Stink bug and ladybug prevention — fall invasion pests
  • Pre-winter perimeter treatment — seal up entry points before cold weather
  • Stored product pest prevention — pantry moths, grain beetles

Winter (December-February)

  • Rodent control — peak mouse and rat activity indoors
  • Cockroach treatments — German roaches thrive in heated homes year-round
  • Bed bug awareness campaigns (post-holiday travel)
  • Quarterly plan promotions — "Start the new year pest-free"

For each campaign, segment your GHL contact list by location, past service type, and plan status. Rodent exclusion campaigns go to homeowners who had any service in the past 18 months. Termite inspection offers target homeowners in neighborhoods with known termite activity. The segmentation makes each campaign relevant rather than spammy.
