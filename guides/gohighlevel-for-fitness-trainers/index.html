How to Set Up GoHighLevel for Your Fitness Business

Step 1: Build Your Client Pipeline

Your pipeline stages mirror how a lead moves from first contact to paying client:

  1. New Lead — filled out a form, called, DM'd, or was referred
  2. Consultation Booked — free intro session or trial class on your calendar
  3. Consultation Completed — met with them, decision pending
  4. Package Sold — purchased training or signed up for membership
  5. Active Client — currently training or on active membership
  6. At Risk — no session booked in 2+ weeks, reactivation triggers
  7. Inactive — no attendance in 6+ weeks

Step 2: Configure Missed Call Text-Back

When you miss a call mid-session, GHL sends: "Hey! This is [Name] at [Business]. I'm with a client right now — are you looking for personal training, group classes, or something else? Text me and I'll get back to you between sessions."

The prospect texts back their goals. GHL creates the contact, logs the conversation, and adds them to your pipeline. The busiest calling windows — 6-8 AM, 12-1 PM, and 5-7 PM — all overlap with peak training hours, making this essential.

Step 3: Build Your Booking Calendar

Set up separate calendars for different appointment types:

  • Free Consultation (30 min): New prospects book directly. Include a pre-session questionnaire about goals, injuries, and availability.
  • Training Session (60 min): Existing clients rebook here. Set buffer time between sessions.
  • Group Class Registration: Capacity limits with automatic waitlist notifications when someone cancels.

Embed the calendar on your website, put the link in your Instagram bio, and include it in every follow-up message.

Step 4: Set Up Appointment Reminders

Create a workflow that triggers when any appointment is confirmed:

  • 24 hours before: "Hey [Name], reminder — your session is tomorrow at [time]. Bring water, wear comfortable shoes. Need to reschedule? Tap here: [link]"
  • 2 hours before: "Your session starts at [time] today at [address]. Let's get after it!"

For free consultations, add a "What to Expect" email 24 hours before. First-timers are nervous — telling them what happens during the session makes them far more likely to show up. This system consistently drops no-show rates from 15-20% to 3-5%.

Automated Follow-Up Sequences That Convert Prospects

The Lead Nurture Sequence

When someone fills out a form, they enter a 7-touch, 14-day automated campaign:

  • Immediate: Text with booking link — "Got your info, excited to learn about your goals. Grab a consultation here: [link]"
  • Day 1: Email with a client success story and before/after results
  • Day 3: Text — "Just checking in. Zero commitment consultation — here's the link: [link]"
  • Day 5: Email — "3 mistakes most beginners make." Genuine value, soft CTA.
  • Day 7: Text — "Still thinking about training? Just want to make sure I'm not bugging you."
  • Day 10: Email — "What one session looks like." Reduce the unknown.
  • Day 14: Text — "Last note. Calendar's here whenever you're ready: [link]"

Conversion rate from a 7-touch sequence: 15-25%. From a single follow-up text: 3-5%.

The Reactivation Campaign

When a client has not booked in 14 days, the contact moves to "At Risk" and a reactivation sequence fires: a friendly check-in text on day 1, a value-driven email on day 5, a "fresh start" offer on day 10, and a final check-in on day 21. These campaigns typically recover 20-30% of lapsed clients. For a trainer losing 3-4 clients per month, recovering even one preserves $640+ in monthly revenue.

Automating Google Reviews

The best time to request a review from a fitness client is after a milestone — a new personal record, completing their first month, or reaching a goal weight. The emotional high produces detailed, glowing reviews.

Set up GHL workflows triggered by session count:

  • After 5th session: "You're five sessions in and the progress is showing. A quick Google review helps other people in [City] find a trainer they can trust. [link]"
  • After completing a package: "Congrats on finishing your pack! A Google review would mean the world. [link]"
  • Every 90 days for active clients: Periodic review request for those who have not left one yet

Trainers running this automation collect 8-15 new reviews per month, transforming their Google presence over a year.

Selling and Managing Training Packages

GHL's funnel builder creates sales pages for your training offerings — starter packages, 12-week transformation programs, monthly unlimited memberships, or small group training. Connect Stripe and buyers purchase directly. Payment triggers your onboarding workflow: welcome email, booking link, intake questionnaire. Automated Onboarding

When a new client signs up, trigger a sequence: welcome text with booking link, pre-session prep email, post-first-session check-in, and milestone texts at sessions 3 and 5. Clients who complete 5 sessions have 90%+ retention rates.

Package Renewal Sequences

At 3 sessions remaining, send a renewal nudge. At 1 session remaining, email a progress recap with renewal options. If the package expires, follow up after 3 days. Automated renewal sequences increase retention by 25-40%.
