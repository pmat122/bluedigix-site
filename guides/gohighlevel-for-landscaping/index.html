Setting Up GoHighLevel for Your Landscaping Business

GHL is a platform, not a plug-and-play app. How you set it up determines how useful it actually is. Here is what the setup looks like for a landscaping company done right.

Build Pipelines Around Your Service Types

Most landscaping companies offer several distinct service categories, and each one has a different sales cycle. A routine lawn maintenance inquiry closes in 24 to 48 hours or it does not close at all. A hardscaping project — a patio, retaining wall, or full backyard redesign — has a weeks-long sales process involving site visits, design consultations, and multiple estimate revisions. Treating these the same way in your CRM is a mistake.

Build separate GHL pipelines for each major service category:

  • Maintenance Pipeline — lawn mowing, fertilization, and weekly service contracts. Fast-close. Automated quote-to-contract flow. Stages: New Lead, Quote Sent, Follow-up 1, Follow-up 2, Closed Won, Closed Lost.
  • Hardscaping Pipeline — patios, walls, fire pits, outdoor kitchens. Longer cycle. Stages: New Lead, Site Visit Scheduled, Proposal In Progress, Proposal Sent, Design Revision, Contract Sent, Signed, Installation Scheduled.
  • Seasonal Services Pipeline — spring cleanup, fall leaf removal, holiday lighting, snow removal. High urgency, highly time-sensitive. Fast automated follow-up is critical. Stages: New Lead, Quote Sent, Booked, Service Complete, Review Requested.
  • Landscape Design Pipeline — full design projects with a design fee upfront. Stages: Discovery Call Booked, Discovery Complete, Design Agreement Sent, Design In Progress, Client Review, Installation Quote Issued.

Having these separated means your automations can be specific. A hardscaping lead gets a different follow-up sequence than someone who wants weekly lawn service. The messaging, timing, and urgency are all different — and your pipeline data will be actually useful when you review it each week.

Automate Estimate Follow-Ups

Sending an estimate and waiting is one of the most expensive habits in the landscaping industry. Customers get busy. They forget. They are not saying no — they just need a nudge. GoHighLevel handles this automatically.

When a contact moves to the "Estimate Sent" stage in your pipeline, the following sequence fires:

  • Day 1 (2 hours after sending): SMS — "Hi [First Name], just wanted to make sure you received the estimate for your [service]. Let me know if you have any questions!"
  • Day 3: Email — A slightly more detailed follow-up asking if they want to schedule a walkthrough or adjust any scope items.
  • Day 7: SMS — "Hi [First Name], wanted to check in on your project. We have some spring availability opening up and want to make sure we can fit you in before the schedule fills."
  • Day 14: Final email — A close or keep-warm message acknowledging the estimate may no longer be active and offering to re-quote if needed.

None of this requires you to remember anything. The moment you log the estimate in GHL, the sequence runs. You get notified only when someone replies — and then you step in to close.

Schedule Seasonal Campaigns in Advance

One of GHL's most underused features for landscaping companies is campaign scheduling. You can build your spring cleanup campaign in December during the slow season and schedule it to send in late February. You can build your snow removal upsell campaign in September and schedule it for the first week of November. Your January self can do the creative work so your October self does not have to.

Seasonal Marketing Workflows With GHL

This is where GoHighLevel creates the most visible difference for landscaping companies. Here is exactly how the seasonal workflow system works across the year.

Spring

In late February, GHL sends a broadcast SMS and email to your entire past-client list: "Spring is three weeks away — we're booking spring cleanups now and slots are limited. Reply YES for a priority estimate or click here to book online."

Past clients who reply enter a booking automation. New contacts who come in from paid ads land on your spring cleanup landing page and go into the same pipeline. The sequence follows up on unsent estimates automatically. By the time March hits and your phone starts ringing from everyone else, your calendar is already partially full with reactivated past clients.

  • Broadcast to past customers at the end of February
  • Automated estimate and booking follow-ups for new inbound leads
  • Calendar fills with your best customers first before you spend a dollar on ads
Summer

Maintenance Contract Upsells (April — June)

Once spring cleanup wraps up, GHL transitions clients who were one-time cleanup customers into recurring maintenance offers. A two-step automation runs: first a thank-you message with a photo or project update, then three days later an offer for a maintenance contract with the first cut free.

This is how one-time customers become weekly maintenance revenue — the most stable and profitable segment of any landscaping business. The conversion rate on these sequences is high because the timing is right (they just had a great experience) and the offer is relevant (they now have a cleaned-up yard to maintain).

  • Post-cleanup conversion sequence to recurring maintenance
  • Hardscaping upsell campaign targeting active maintenance clients
  • Mid-summer check-in to identify add-on services (mulch refresh, fertilization, irrigation checks)
Fall

Leaf Removal and Annual Contract Renewal (September — October)

Fall has two goals: sell leaf removal to your existing base and lock in next year's annual contracts before the off-season. GHL handles both with separate but overlapping campaigns.

The leaf removal campaign broadcasts in early September. The annual contract renewal campaign runs in October, targeting your full maintenance client list with an early-renewal offer — a locked-in rate for next year's services if they sign before December 1st. This is the move that protects your revenue pipeline heading into winter and ensures you start next spring with a full route instead of starting from zero.

  • Leaf removal broadcast to all active clients and past-season customers
  • Annual contract renewal campaign with early-sign incentive
  • Holiday lighting upsell (if offered) goes out to premium property clients
Winter

Snow Removal Upsells (October — November)

For landscaping companies in northern markets, snow removal is the difference between a profitable winter and a break-even one. GHL runs a snow removal campaign targeting your active maintenance client list — these are your warmest prospects because they already trust you with their property.

The sequence offers seasonal snow removal contracts with priority service guarantees. It follows up twice. It tracks who expressed interest but did not convert and sends them a final offer before the first storm. Existing customers who add snow removal service have the highest average lifetime value of any segment in the landscaping industry.

  • Snow removal broadcast to maintenance clients in October
  • Two-touch follow-up for non-converts before first snow forecast
  • New-client snow removal landing page for paid acquisition

The compounding effect: When these seasonal campaigns run every year, each year gets easier. Your past-client list grows. Your review count grows. Your referral network expands. By year three, a meaningful portion of your spring schedule fills from automated reactivation campaigns before you spend anything on new lead generation.
