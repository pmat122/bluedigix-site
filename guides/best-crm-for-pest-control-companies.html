What to Look for in a Pest Control CRM

1. Industry-Specific Workflows

A generic CRM won't cut it in pest control. You need a system that understands your business model: initial inspection → quote → treatment schedule → recurring service → upsell opportunities. The CRM should automate these workflows so your sales reps and field teams don't have to manually track every step.

2. Automated Follow-Ups and Reminders

When a homeowner calls at 6 PM with a pest problem, your office is closed. Without automation, that lead goes cold. The best CRM for pest control automatically sends appointment reminders, quote follow-ups, and service confirmations via text and email—24/7, even when your team is offline.

3. Field Service Integration

Your technicians are in the field. They need mobile access to customer history, treatment details, photos from past inspections, and the ability to close jobs on the spot. A CRM with strong field service features eliminates office paperwork and speeds up invoicing.

4. Scheduling and Route Optimization

Pest control is geography-dependent. If you're scheduling treatments across a city or region, a CRM that maps routes, clusters appointments, and reduces travel time saves hours per week and fuel costs. It also reduces no-shows because customers get automated reminders of the appointment window.

5. Recurring Revenue Management

Pest control thrives on recurring contracts—monthly preventative treatments, quarterly inspections, seasonal packages. Your CRM must handle subscription billing, automate renewal notices, and flag customers nearing contract end dates so your team can upsell before they churn.

6. Lead Scoring and Prioritization

Not all leads are equal. A customer calling about a one-time termite inspection has different value than a property manager needing year-round pest management for a 20-unit building. A smart CRM scores leads and flags high-value prospects so your sales team focuses on deals worth closing.

7. Integration with Marketing Tools

You need your CRM to sync with your email, text, and social media marketing. When a customer abandons a quote, an automated email sequence follows up. When a customer's contract is expiring, a text reminder goes out. These integrations drive revenue without adding manual work.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Not using automation. Buying a CRM but manually following up defeats the purpose. Set up automated text and email sequences. Let the system do the work.

Mistake 2: Not training your team. A CRM is only as good as your adoption. If your field techs and office staff don't know how to use it, data gets messy and the system fails.

Mistake 3: Using a generic CRM. Salesforce is powerful but not designed for pest control. You need a system that speaks your language: inspections, treatments, recurring contracts, seasonality.

Mistake 4: Ignoring mobile access. Your team is in the field. If the CRM isn't mobile-first, it won't get used. Ensure full functionality on phones and tablets.

Mistake 5: Not integrating with payment processing. Collecting payment at the job site (not after) increases conversion and cash flow. Make sure your CRM handles this.
