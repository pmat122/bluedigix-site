But most pet owners don't rebook on the spot — they say "I'll call when it's time" and then forget until the coat is matted again. Automated rebooking reminders solve this by reaching out at the right interval with a one-tap booking link.

Reviews Drive Local Discovery

How to Set Up GoHighLevel for Your Grooming Business

Step 1: Build Your Grooming Pipeline

  1. New Inquiry — someone called, texted, messaged, or submitted a booking form
  2. Consultation / Booked — first-time clients may need a breed assessment; returning clients go straight to booked
  3. Confirmed — appointment confirmed via reminder reply
  4. Groomed — appointment completed; review request and rebooking reminder trigger
  5. Recurring Client — client is on a regular grooming schedule
  6. Lost / Inactive — hasn't booked in 12+ weeks

If you offer both grooming and additional services (daycare, boarding, dental cleaning, spa treatments), you may want separate pipelines or at least clear tagging to track which services each client uses.

Step 2: Turn On Missed Call Text-Back

Configure your message: "Hey there! This is [Salon Name]. Sorry we missed your call — we're with a pup right now. Text us your dog's name, breed, and what they need (bath, full groom, nail trim, etc.) and we'll text back with availability! 🐾"

The pet owner texts: "Max, golden retriever, needs a full groom. Coat is getting pretty matted." GHL creates the contact, logs the conversation, adds them to your pipeline. When you finish your current dog, you respond with availability and pricing. That booking request is captured instead of lost.

For grooming, the busiest calling times are early morning (pet owners calling before work) and lunch hour — both of which overlap with your grooming schedule. Missed call text-back is critical during these windows.

Step 3: Set Up Appointment Reminders

This is the no-show prevention system. Create a workflow that triggers when a booking is confirmed:

  • 48 hours before: Text — "Hi [Name]! Just a friendly reminder that [Pet Name] has a grooming appointment at [Salon] on [day] at [time]. Reply YES to confirm or RESCHEDULE if you need to change. See you soon!"
  • 24 hours before: Text — "Quick reminder — [Pet Name]'s groom is tomorrow at [time]. Please arrive 5-10 minutes early. If [Pet Name] has any skin issues, hot spots, or matting you'd like us to know about, text us now so we're prepared."
  • Morning of: Text — "Today's the day! [Pet Name] is scheduled for [time] at [Salon]. See you soon! Address: [address]. Any last-minute questions, text or call us."

The morning-of text with the address is especially helpful for new clients. The 24-hour text asking about skin issues or matting is a nice touch that shows professionalism and helps you prepare for the groom. These reminders consistently reduce no-shows from 10-15% down to 2-3%.

Step 4: Automate Review Requests

Pet grooming has a unique advantage for reviews: emotional timing. When a pet owner picks up their freshly groomed dog — clean, fluffy, looking adorable — they're experiencing a burst of happiness. That's the perfect moment for a review request.

  • Trigger: Contact moves to "Groomed" stage
  • Wait: 2 hours (enough time for pickup and the "aww" moment at home)
  • Send text: "Hope [Pet Name] is loving their fresh groom! If you're happy with how [he/she] looks, we'd really appreciate a quick Google review. It helps other pet parents in [City] find great grooming. [review link] Thank you!"
  • Wait: 3 days
  • Send text: "Quick reminder — if you have a sec, a Google review would mean the world to our little salon. [link] 🐾"

Personalizing with the pet's name and using warm, casual language matches the tone pet owners expect from their groomer. Groomers running this system consistently add 8-15 new reviews per month.

Step 5: Build Breed-Specific Rebooking Reminders

This is where the recurring revenue gets locked in. Different breeds need grooming at different intervals:

  • Short coat (Labs, Beagles, Boxers): 6-8 week reminder
  • Medium coat (Cocker Spaniels, Border Collies): 5-6 week reminder
  • Long coat (Shih Tzus, Yorkies, Maltese): 4-5 week reminder
  • Double coat (Huskies, Golden Retrievers, Pomeranians): 6-8 weeks, with seasonal deshedding add-on pitches
  • Doodle/Poodle mixes: 4-6 week reminder (high-maintenance coats that mat quickly)

Tag each client by breed category after their first groom. Create a workflow for each tag that waits the appropriate interval and sends a rebooking text: "Hey [Name], it's been about [X] weeks since [Pet Name]'s last groom. Time for a refresh? Here's our booking link to grab a spot this week: [link]. Can't wait to see [Pet Name] again!"

If they don't rebook within a week, a follow-up text goes out: "Just a friendly nudge — [breed type] coats start getting tangled around the [X]-week mark. Booking now keeps [Pet Name] comfortable and makes the groom easier (and faster). [link]"

This system is what separates groomers with a waitlist from groomers with empty slots. When 80% of your clients rebook automatically through these reminders, you barely need to market for new customers.

Seasonal Campaigns That Fill Your Calendar

Spring (March-May)

  • Spring shedding season — deshedding packages for double-coated breeds
  • Flea and tick season prep — clean grooming + prevention product recommendations
  • "Spring refresh" packages at a slight discount to reactivate lapsed clients

Summer (June-August)

  • Summer cuts — shorter trims for heat relief
  • Puppy season — new puppy owner first-groom specials (builds lifetime clients)
  • Swim season — post-swim conditioning treatments for dogs who play in pools and lakes

Fall (September-November)

  • Holiday prep — "Get gorgeous for family photos" grooming packages
  • Fall coat transition — conditioning treatments as coats thicken
  • Gift card promotions for holiday giving

Winter (December-February)

  • Holiday grooming — book early for Thanksgiving/Christmas family events
  • Paw care — moisturizing treatments for dry, cracked pads from salt and cold
  • "New year, new grooming schedule" — push lapsed clients to rebook and commit to a regular schedule

For each campaign, GHL sends targeted texts to the relevant segment. Deshedding campaigns go to owners of double-coated breeds. Puppy specials go to contacts tagged as "puppy" or acquired recently. Holiday grooming reminders go to your full active client list. The personalization makes each campaign feel like a message from their groomer, not a mass blast.
